Jeremy Scott Seeks Inspiration From The Sims For Latest Moschino Collection

 

 

The designer taps into pop culture nostalgia once again.

15/04/2019

It's official: Moschino's creative director Jeremy Scott has reached peak millennial courtesy of his latest collection inspired by The Sims. In sartorial homage to the life simulation game, Scott has created a ready-to-wear line which features signature Moschino motifs such as the teddy bear and retro chains - all warped through a noughties Microsoft lens.

The PC-inspired line even plays on the plumbob (the green diamond which floats over each Sim's head) with a thigh-cut swimsuit and gram-worthy umbrella.

Even The Sims will be finally be able to upgrade their pixelated wardrobe in one fell swoosh thanks to the Freezer Bunny hoodie. For the campaign, iPhone-clutching models, Stella Maxwell, Aiden and Denek K, have even been plonked into the make-believe world in a bid to bring 'avatar realness'.

'I love the idea of being able to imagine, design and bring to life a world of individual personas with The Sims universe,' Scott said. 'That concept emulates what I get to do for each collection at Moschino as I create a fantasy universe of spectacular storylines and characters.'

 

Though the designer is no stranger to tapping into pop culture nostalgia, as his latest runway show paid tribute to '70s game shows such as The Price is Right and Wheel of Fortune.

Models Bella Hadid, Stella Maxwell, Kaia Gerber and Joan Smalls dolled up as show hosts on the runway with heavily-lacquered hair and gaudy make-up. The Moschino x The Sims collaboration comes a year ahead of the EA game's 20th anniversary and is now available to shop
